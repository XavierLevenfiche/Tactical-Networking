# PACE Communication Planning

## Overview

Reliable communication systems often rely on **multiple redundant methods** rather than a single network.

A common framework used in communications planning is the **PACE model**.

PACE stands for:

Primary
Alternate
Contingency
Emergency

---

## Communication Layers

### Primary

The preferred communication method used under normal conditions.

Example:

* primary radio network
* primary data network

---

### Alternate

A secondary communication method used if the primary system fails.

Example:

* secondary radio channel
* alternate network link

---

### Contingency

A backup communication method used if both the primary and alternate systems fail.

Example:

* text messaging
* secondary radio device

---

### Emergency

The final method used when all other communications have failed.

Example:

* direct voice contact
* physical signals
* fallback emergency frequency

---

## Relevance to Networking Research

Understanding PACE planning highlights an important concept:

Reliable communications are not built around **one technology**, but around **multiple layered systems**.

This insight will guide further research into decentralized networking and tactical communication systems.
